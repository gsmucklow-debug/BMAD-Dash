### 1. The "Any Project" Promise
The core objective was to prove BMAD Dash isn't just for building itself.
*   **Echo-OS Success**: We successfully loaded the Echo-OS project (F:/Echo-OS) with 100% feature parity. The dashboard correctly identified its phase, stories, and evidence gaps without manual configuration.
*   **Universal Parsing**: Hardened the `BMADParser` to handle hyphenated (`5-1`) and decimal (`5.1`) story ID formats seamlessly.

### 2. Zero-Configuration Context Loading
Improved the UX for multi-project workflows:
*   **URL Parameter Support**: Implemented `?project_root=` parameter handling, allowing instant context switching via direct links.
*   **Isolation Guarantee**: Validated that the `ProjectStateCache` and `SmartCache` correctly isolate data between projects, preventing cross-pollination of evidence or status.

### 3. Resilience & Graceful Degradation
Hardened the system against "The Messy Middle" of project development:
*   **Parser Robustness**: The dashboard now survives missing `_bmad-output` directories, malformed YAML, and incomplete artifacts by degrading gracefully to "Unknown" states rather than crashing.
*   **Error Documentation**: Delivered a comprehensive `Error Handling & Graceful Degradation Guide` for future maintainers.

## ðŸ§  Lessons Learned

*   **URL vs LocalStorage**: Relying solely on `localStorage` for the project root was a mismatch for a tool intended for multi-project monitoring. URL parameters provide much better shareability and bookmarking.
*   **Regex Flexibility**: Minor variations in story ID naming (periods vs hyphens) were the single biggest risk to cross-project compatibility. Standardizing on a flexible regex was a critical stabilization step.
*   **Cache Warming**: Initial load on Echo-OS was slower (~1.2s) than the BMAD Dash baseline (~300ms) due to the first-time bootstrap. We learned that for large projects, cache-warming or progressive parsing might be needed to maintain the <500ms NFR.
